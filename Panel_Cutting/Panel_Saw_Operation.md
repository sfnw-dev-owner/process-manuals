# Panel Saw Operation

1. The panel saw can cut in two directions: horizontally or
   vertically. The operation is slightly different for these two kinds
   of cuts, so it is important to keep in mind which one you are
   performing.

1. In all cases, let the saw do the cutting; do not force it through
   the material, or the material through the saw. Check each piece of
   paneling for staples: running one staple through the saw will chip
   a carbide tooth, and soon the blade will be worthless.

1. Before loading the first panel onto the saw carriage, TURN ON the
   dust collector. The switch for this is behind the saw frame on the
   left, where it will be inaccessible once you load a panel onto the
   saw.

1. For horizontal cuts, pull the two black knobs on the saw turntable
   toward you, and rotate the table so that the arrow labeled FEED
   points to the left. When you release the locking knobs, they should
   snap into place, and the table should not rotate any further. Then,
   set the height of the cut, either with a jig or by using the
   (hidden) scale at the left of the saw table. Lock down the carriage
   by tightening the black handles on each round guide rail.

1. For vertical cuts, pull the two black knobs on the saw turntable
   and rotate the table so that the arrow labeled FEED points
   DOWN. Loosen the locking handles on the guide rails, so that the
   saw table is free to move vertically, and raise it all the way up.

# Roof Panels

1. The material for the exterior is ¾" CDX plywood. Neither side is
   very pretty, but one is usually worse. That side should face you
   when sawing roof panels.

1. You will only need to make vertical cuts for this process. Set a
   block to act as a saw stop for 77 ¾"

1. Cut 4 panels for each home, placing them on an orange panel
   cart. Generally, put a maximum of 8 panels on a cart.

1. Secure the panels to the cart with a strap before moving the cart.

# Exterior Panels

1. The material for the exterior is T1-11 siding. It is fairly
   flexible, and requires some care to make sure it is kept flat as it
   is being sawed.

1. You will cut two homes' worth of panels at once, and should place
   two orange panel carts by the panel saw.

1. Cut all panels from 96" to 93½" using the jig labeled "T1-11
   Exterior Panel 93½".  The jig has feet labeled 1 and 5, to be
   placed in the saw carriage's slots 1 (unlabled) and 5. Ensure blue
   tape marks line up.

1. Make sure the saw is set for vertical cuts, and that the saw table
   is all the way up and the vacuum is on. Place a panel (good side
   away from you) on the panel saw rollers on the left carriage, and
   slide the panel into the saw until it touches the panel-stop.

1. Turn on the saw (green button), place a foot on the panel OUTSIDE
   the left guide rail, flattening the panel, and lower the saw table
   to cut the panel. When you reach the bottom, have one person pull
   the panel away from the blade as another pulls the off-cut up to
   free the blade, and turn the saw off. The off-cut is trash.

1. Remove the panel, and place it on one of the panel carts.

1. Each home needs 8 solid panels and 2 panels with windows cut out of
   them. When cutting panels for two homes, load one cart with 12
   panels and one with 8.

1. There is a separate jig for window cutouts. For this process, you
   will need a router, a drill with a spade bit in it, and a broom and
   dustpan. (also two clamps which live in the jig)

1. Move the 12-panel cart you have designated for window panels up
   fairly close to the jig, and bring the other cart next to it.

1. Place a panel from the 12-panel cart on the jig, right side up, and
   with the tongue against the stop. (The tongue is the thinner edge
   extending the not-pretty side of the panel). Make sure the panel
   slides under the blocks at the jig's closed corner, and seats both
   along the long tongue edge and at the top of the panel. Clamp the
   panel using the fixed clamps, and also use 2 clamps where it says
   CLAMP on the jig.

1. Bore an access hole where it is indicated by two arrows on the jig,
   and route out the window.

1. Unclamp the panel and place it on the 8-panel cart.

1. Repeat steps 10-12 four times. You should end up with a total of 8
   solid panels and 2 window panels on each home-panel cart. Re-secure
   these panels to their cart.

1. Sweep up as much sawdust as possible and vacuum the jig.

# Interior Panels

- The material for the interior is 15/32" plywood. The good side goes
  against the carriage when sawing.

- You will need one orange panel cart for each home's panels, and one
  extra one, to hold panels temporarily.

- Follow the **Interior Panels Cut List** laminated sheet which details
  the cuts that will be made.

- Horizontal cuts: There are jigs to set the saw table height for each
  panel size. To use one, set the end marked DOWN on the yellow tabs
  at the bottom of the saw frame between the two guide rails, and then
  lower the saw table until the bottom edge of the table rests on the
  ledge of the jig. The words "tuck under bottom of saw carriage"
  should NOT go under the table, but rest in front of the saw
  table. Lock the saw table in place, and remove the jig.
  Make all the horizontal cuts described on the cut list first.

- Vertical cuts: Insert the correct panel-stop with the feet between
  the feed rollers on the right carriage of the panel saw, so that
  slot numbers match and the blue tape on the panel-stop matches the
  blue tape on the panel saw carriage.

- All markings should be made in PENCIL, and about a foot in from the
  top-left corner.

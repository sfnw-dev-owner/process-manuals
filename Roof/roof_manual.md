# Material Preparation

## Tar Paper
 - Cut 4 lengths at 13'.  Roll each length and secure with blue tape
 - If the end of a roll is too short, add a second piece long enough to reach 13' and then another 12", to allow for overlap between the two parts.

## Metal Flashing
 - We need two end pieces and two eave coverings, which will be in two parts.
 - The end flashing is 115" long.  Cut from a 10' stick, and then cut the outer edge only at 52-1/2".  The outer edge is the one with a 1/2" flange.
 Do not cut through the flat half of the flashing.
 - Eave flashing requires two pieces: a 10' solid piece, and a 44" piece.
 - Bundle these 6 pieces together and secure with a bungee-cord loop around them.

## Shingle Prep
  To insure that seams between shingles do not line up and permit water to penetrate the shingling, we create start and end shingles of different lengths.  In between, for each course, we need 3 full-length shingles.  The length of the start plus end shingles is 41".

### Starter Course
 - This row uses special single-layer shingle material.  We need 16 pieces for a full roof.

### First 8 Rows
 - Cut a shingle to 24" to start.
 - Line it up with the stop on the shingle cutter, and place a second shingle next to it on the cutter.
 - Cut the second shingle.  The two adjacent pieces are a start/end pair.  Stack them, and move the remaining piece to the stop.
 - Repeat the process using the scrap and a whole shingle, for a total of 2 sets of 10 pairs.
 - Separate the top 2 pairs in each set and use them for the next two steps.

### Row 9
 - This row needs the top 3" cut off, so that the remaining shingle reaches just to the edge of the vent block openings.
 - Cut 6 full shingles down by this much, and cut two start/end pairs down the same way.

### Final Row
 - This row needs the top half cut off, so that its top edge matches the top edge of Row 9, and does not obscure the vent block opening.
 - Cut 6 full shingles down just below the adhesive strip, and cut two start/end pairs down the same way.

### Ridge Cap
 - These 28-30 small shingles are supplied as tear-offs, 3 to a sheet.
 - We need 10 sheets.

## Ridge Vent
 - Total length needed is 13'-4".
 - Cut two pieces to make up this length,.
 - Secure them with bungee cord loops.

# Roof Installation

## Setting up

 - Close the drain stopper on the bottom of the compressor.
 - Turn on air compressor.  There is a switch on the power strip strapped to a shelf corner leg above the compressor.
 - Unlock the nailers from the skymobile.
 - Add 3 drops of air gun oil into the compressed air connector on the framing nailer, and attach the air hose to it.
 - Collect all the prepared roofing material:
 	- Tar paper rolls
 	- Metal Flashing bundle
 	- Starter Row shingle bundle
 	- Start/End Shingle sets
 	- Ridge Vent
 	- Tear-off Cap shingle set
 	- Rolls of Flexible Flashing
 	- 2 or 3 bundles of full-length shingles
 	- Pre-cut 3/4" plywood
 	- 4 Fascia Boards with 18-1/2° cut on one end.  You need 2 left-hand and 2 right-hand pieces
 	- 4 Soffet Boards (1x6) precut with 18-1/2° bevel on one end
  - There should be framing nails, roofing nails, and hurricane clips on the skymobiles
 
## Plywood Deck
 - There are two options for 3/4" plywood installation: either install two 77-3/4" sheets on a side of the roof, or install a full 96" sheet with two shorter 29-3/4" pieces, one on each side of the full sheet.
 - In the second case, use hurricane clips on the seams, to insure that the roof deck is a single plane.
 - Position the plywood tight against the 1x8 white board at the bottom of the half roof.
 - Snap chalk lines to show where the rafters under the plywood are: use the screw heads on the white board and the visible end of the rafter at the peak as references.
 - Nail the plywood to the rafters every 8", using framing nails.
 - Repeat on the other side of the home.

`#pagebreak()`{=typst}

## Fascia Boards
 - With one person holding the fascia board from the roof and another on a ladder, position one fascia board on the back
of the home so that it is flush with the top of the plywood, and its upper end is exactly on the parting line between the
two 1x6 boards that form the ridge beam.  Using panel nails, nail fascia board to the rafter behind it.
 - Line up the second fascia board on the other side of the roof at the back of the home so that it is flush with the roof deck and so that the center seam is tight.  Nail this board to the rafter behind it, with 2 nails an inch from each end, and one nail every 8" - 10" between.

## Soffets
 - From a ladder, position each soffet board so that it is tight against the ridge beam (white boards at the peak) and against the T1-11 siding.  If the soffet is too wide, mark where it needs to be trimmed, and trim on the table saw.  There is a sled that will allow you to adjust the angle of the cut from one end to the other, so that this cut can be made safely.
 - Using panel nails, nail the soffet into the cross blocks between the end rafter and the rafter resting on the end wall.

## Metal Flashing
 - Prepare the coil nailer.  Add 10-15 drops of gun oil into the compressed-air fitting, and connect the air hose to the coil nailer.  Load short roofing nails into the coil magazine, after adjusting the coil platform to the upper position.
 - At one eave, place a 10' length of metal flashing against the edge of the white board flush with the outer edge of the rear fascia board,
and nail through the eave whiteboard into the rafters, using the coil nailer.
 - Place a short piece of flashing against the edge of the white board flush with the outer edge of the front fascia board and overlapping the long flashing.  Nail this piece through the whiteboard into the rafters, and nail once through the end of the short flashing (this nail will show through the whiteboard, but that cannot be helped.)
 - Place one of the long pieces of metal flashing that has been partially cut in the middle so that it extends over the peak from one eave
to the other, and overlaps the end of the eave flashing.  Overlap the vertical parts of the flashing at the peak.  If one end hangs over
beyond the eave flashing, trim it with metal shears.
 - Nail the end flashing to the roof decking below, about 1-1/4" from the outer edge of the flashing.
 - Repeat on the other end of the roof.

`#pagebreak()`{=typst}

## Flexible Flashing
- At each eave, peel the backing paper off the first foot or two of a roll of flexible flashing, and position the
  flashing nearly even with the end flashing, and about 1/4" up from the outer edge of the eave flashing.  Press this
  section down, and then, with a helper, roll out more material from the roll, set the flashing approximately where you
  want it, and then pull the brown paper out sideways from under the flashing, pressing the flashing into place as you
  go.
 - Cut the flexible flashing to length at the other end of the eave, and press the last length down onto both the metal flashing and the whiteboard.
 - Repeat on the other eave.
 - At the corner on one side of the house, start a line of flexible flashing up the end of the roof toward the peak, and extend
 this all the way over the peak and down the opposite side of the roof, ending with a full overlap of the horizontal run of flexible flashing.
 - Repeat on the other end.

## Tar Paper
 - Find two roofing gauges, to measure the overhang of tar paper and shingles.
 - Unroll a length of tar paper on the lower part of the roof, and position it so that the tar paper extends 1" beyond
 he metal flashing on each end of the roll, and 1" below the eave flashing.  The roofing gauges make thisit easier to check the overhang.
 - Staple the tar paper to the roof plywood: 3 rows of staples about 12" apart, one near the top of the sheet, one in the middle,
 and one about 3" above the bottom of the sheet, to avoid stapling into the metal flashing.
 - Unroll a second length of tar paper and position it again with 1" overhang on each end, and lined up at the top with the upper edge
 of the plywood, keeping the vent openings clear.  This sheet will overlap the lower tar paper sheet.
 - Staple this sheet in the same manner as the lower sheet.
 - Repeat on the other side of the home.

## Shingles
 - Underlay Course
     - Use single layer starter shingles.
     - Starting at the front of the home, position a starter shingle parallel to the eave edge hanging over 1" beyond the front fascia and 1" below the eave edge.
    - Nail 3 places just above the black adhesive in the middle of the shingle.
    - Similarly position 2 more starter shingles, maintaining the 1" overhang at the eave, and butted up against the previous shingle.  Nail these in place.
    - Position a 4th starter shingle overhanging the rear fascia by 1" and extending 1" below the eave edge.  Nail it in place.
    - Cut a small piece of scrap to fill in the 2"-3" gap, and hail that in place.
    - Repeat on the other side of the home.
    - Position a starter shingle running up the roof, butted against the one at the eave, and hanging 1" beyond the outer edge of the rear fascia.  Nail it in place.
    - Repeat, running a row of these starter shingles all the way over the peak, and ending at the eave row on the other side.  Do not allow this row to overlap the eave shingles; trim the last one as necessary.
    - Similarly, place and nail a row of starter shingles at the front of the roof.
 - First 8 Courses
    - Separate the start and end shingles, making sure that each pair amounts to the same length.
    - Set the first start shingle so that it extends 1" beyond the outer edge of the front fascia board and 1" below the eave edge. Nail it in place.
    - Set three full shingles, each butted up against the previous one, and each extending 1" below the eave edge.  Nail these in place.
    - The first end shingle should then fill the space left in this row, and extend 1" beyond the outer edge of the rear fascia board. Nail it in place.
    - For each succeeding row, set the start shingle so that its lower edge lines up with the cutout pattern in the shingle under it, and with a 1" overhang of the front fascia.  Set three full shingles next, also lined up with their bottom edges even with the cutout pattern on the shingles below them.  The ending shingle for the row should then fill the remaining space in that row at the back of the home.
    - There should be no nail heads visible, except in the 8th row, where they will be covered up in the next step.  If there are any, that is a mistake.  You will have to come back and caulk them after the shingling is complete.

`#pagebreak()`{=typst}

- 9th Course
    - Using the shingles trimmed down for this course, position the start shingle overhanging the front fascia by 1", as before,
but for this row, align the *top* of the shingle with the top edge of the plywood (or tar paper).  Nail the start shingle down.
    - Positiion and nail down the 3 full shingles for this row, each butted against the previous one, and with the top even with the edge of the plywood.
    - The end shingle for this row should fill the space left, and hang over the rear fascia by 1".
- 10th Course
    - Using the half-height shingles, position the start shingle so that it hangs over the front fascia by 1" and is parallel with the top edge
of the plywood.  Nail it in place.
    - Position and nail down the 3 full-width shingles for this row, each butted against the previous one, and with the top even with the edge of the plywood.
    - The end shingle for this row should fill the space left, and hang over the rear fascia by 1".
     
## Ridge Vent
- Once both sides of the roof are shingled, install the roof vent.
    - Remove the remaining coil of short roofing nails from the coil nailer, reset the coil platform to its lower position,
and insert a coil of longer roofing nails.
    - At each end of the home, staple a plug of foam insulation over the double rafter ends at the peak, to seal this gap.
    - Prepare the long section of ridge vent, by gluing a foam plug into the space between the two honeycomb sections.
Spread a line of exterior caulk on the orange side of the foam plug, and press this into the space at one end of the ridge vent.
    - Spread two lines of caulk on the under side of the vent, following the stitching that holds the pink fabric to the vent.
    - With a helper, fold the vent partly (to make is stiffer) and position this section of vent so that it is centered on the ridge
and hangs over the rear of the home about 2" beyond the fascia (1" longer than the shingles.)  Press down to make sure the caulk adheres
to the shingles, and nail on each side of the vent about 1" in from the edge and about 12" apart.
    - Trial-fit the short piece of vent, to make sure it extends about 2" forward from the front fascia, and trim it if necessary.
    - Install a foam plug in the forward end of this section (as above), and spread caulk lines down both sides of this section.
    - Position this section so that it butts against the previously installed section of vent, and is centered on the ridge.
    - Nail it in place.

## Cap Shingles
- If these tear-off shingles are not already separated into three 13"x13" pieces, fold each one along the perforations and tear them into this size,
- Starting at the rear of the home, place a cap shingle even with the end of the ridge vent and centered on it from side to side.
- Nail in two places, about 2" in from each side and just above the black adhesive stripe,
- Overlap the next cap shingle so that it covers the adhesive strip on the previous one and is centered side-to-side on the ridge vent. Nail it in place.
- Repeat for a total of 14 cap shingles.  This should bring you near the middle of the ridge.
- Begin again at the front of the home, setting the first cap shingle even with the end of the ridge vent, and overlapping cap shingles until you reach the line from the rear of the home.
- Install the last shingle of the front row so that it overlaps the last one from the back row.
- Cut one more shingle just below the adhesive line, and install it over the seam between the front and rear halves of the cap.  Nail this final
cap shingle in four places.
- Caulk these four nail heads, being sure that the caulk seals onto the shingle material on all sides of the nail.  To improve the look, sprinkle a little of the shingle dust on each caulk spot, so that in blends in with the color of the rest of the roof.
